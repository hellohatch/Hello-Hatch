# Leadership Risk Intelligence™ Platform Standards

This document standardizes scoring, interpretation, report structure, and advisory output behavior across the Leadership Risk Intelligence™ platform.

## 1) Assessment Standards

- Response scale is fixed and required:
  - `1` Rarely true for me
  - `2` Occasionally true for me
  - `3` Sometimes true for me
  - `4` Often true for me
  - `5` Consistently true for me
- Assessment includes two components:
  - Leadership Signal Index™ (LSI)
  - Leadership Load Index™ (LLI)
- Scale measures behavioral frequency, not subjective agreement.

## 2) Domain Definition Standards

LSI domains are fixed:

- Stress Regulation
- Cognitive Breadth
- Trust Climate
- Ethical Integrity
- Leadership Durability
- Adaptive Capacity

Definitions must remain consistent across organizations and reports.

## 3) Scoring Standards

- Domain score = average of assigned question set.
- LSI and LLI scores are each in `1..5`.
- Concentration Exposure Index™ (CEI) is computed with the standardized CEI algorithm.
- Leadership Risk Score™ is computed with the standardized formula.
- Outputs must not vary by report writer.

## 4) Report Structure Standards

Executive Insight Brief™ sections are fixed and ordered:

1. Executive Structural Overview
2. The Environment You Are Operating In
3. Leadership Signal Profile
4. Domain Interpretation and Analysis
5. Snapshot of Risk Before Anything Breaks
6. Six to Twelve Month Structural Projection
7. Concentration Exposure Index
8. Leadership Cost Cascade Placement
9. Thirty Day Structural Strengthening Plan
10. Organizational Implications
11. Final Perspective

Appendix is fixed:

- Appendix A: Advisory Integration Pathway
- Appendix B: Leadership Signal Definitions

## 5) Report Tone Standards

- Write directly to leader (`you`, `your leadership`, `your role`, `your decision environment`).
- Use executive advisory language.
- Avoid clinical or academic voice.

## 6) Paragraph Structure Standards

- Most paragraphs: 3 to 7 sentences.
- Short one-sentence emphasis paragraphs: use sparingly (max ~3 to 4 per section).

## 7) Interpretation Standards

Interpretations must connect signals to real leadership context:

- Durability → sustained workload and decision demand
- Cognitive Breadth → strategy discussion and option exploration
- Trust Climate → speaking up and dissent quality

Avoid abstract psychological framing.

## 8) Visualization Standards

Every report must include:

- Leadership Signal Radar Chart
- Concentration Exposure Index graphic
- Leadership Cost Cascade placement

Design language must remain consistent across reports.

## 9) Cost Cascade Placement Standards

Placement must mirror CEI stage exactly:

- Healthy Distribution
- Exposure
- Concentration
- Structural Risk

## 10) Advisory Output Standards

Sections 9–11 must translate signals into concrete leadership implications and actions for the specific profile; avoid generic recommendations.

## 11) Consistency Standard

All outputs (analyst, AI, automation) must apply identical scoring, interpretation, structure, and visualization standards.

## Leadership Mirror Effect (Writing Rule)

Each interpretation must answer:

1. What signal is present
2. How it appears in the leader’s week
3. How organization/team experience it

Every domain interpretation should include three anchors:

- Leadership Experience
- Team Experience
- Organizational Impact

Sections where Mirror Effect should be strongest:

- Domain Interpretation
- Snapshot of Risk Before Anything Breaks
- 30 Day Structural Strengthening Plan
- Organizational Implications
- Final Perspective

---

## Narrative Engine Standard

Each domain section follows five fixed layers:

1. What this signal measures
2. What your score suggests
3. What this likely looks like in your role
4. What signal compression would look like
5. Why it matters for the organization

This structure is mandatory for consistency and enterprise reliability.
